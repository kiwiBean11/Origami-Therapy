Mental Health and Origami

Taking care of your mental health is vital, as it is the foundation for your ability to think, feel, interact with others, and cope with life's challenges. Self-care is not a luxury but a necessary practice of prioritizing your well-being to prevent burnout and maintain resilience. Engaging in simple, focused activities like origami is an excellent, accessible form of self-care. Below is some evidence on how practicing origami can significantly benefit mental health:

1. It can promote mindfulness and reduce stress by focusing the mind's attention on a specific task. This helps to limit the untrusive or anxious thoughts that may temporarily invade your mind. It also helps relaxation levels by lowering blood pressure and reducing a sense of anxiety and stress that one may have.

2. It can boost self esteem and your sense of control. When you successfully master a new piece you have a visible reward. This contributes to your sense of accomplishment and a feeling of achievement from completing a challenging task. Additionally, by transforming a 2D piece of paper into a 3D artwork, it allows you to feel a sense of control over your surroundings, a lack of which may contribute to anxious thoughts.

3. Origami Enhances COgnitive and Emotional Resilience. Copmleting the complex models that origami can create takes dedication and the patience to accept that msitakes will be made, growing your sense of patience and persistence. It also adds to your ability to solve problems, as folding the precise geometric patterns stimulates the brain.
